Anthony Enaharo

Father of Nigeria State

Here's a time line of Anthony Enahoro:

  • 22nd of July 1923 -Chief Anthony Enahoro was born, he was one of Nigeria's foremost anti-colonial and pro-democracy activists.

  • Enahoro had a long and distinguished career in the press, politics, civil service and the pro-democracy movement.

  • He attended Government School, Uromi, Government School, Owo and King's College, Lagos.

  • 1944 - Enahoro became the editor of Nnamdi Azikiwe's newspaper, the Southern Nigerian Defender, Ibadan, at the age of 21, thus becoming Nigeria's youngest editor ever.

  • 1945 to 1949 - He was the editor of Zik's Comet, Kano.

  • 1950 to 1953 - he was one of the associate editor of West African Pilot, Lagos, and editor-in-chief of Morning Star.

  • 1953 - Enahoro became the first to move the motion for Nigeria's independence.

  • 1960 - The motion which he moved was eventually granted in, and after several political setbacks and defeats in the parliament. Enahoro has been regarded by academics and many Nigerians, as the "Father of Nigeria State.

  • 1967–1974 - He became Federal Commissioner (Minister) for Information and Labour under the General Yakubu Gowon Military Government.
  • 1975 - He was the Federal Commissioner for Special Duties.

  • 1978–83 - He became a member of the National Party of Nigeria. NPN.

  • 1972–75 - He was the president, World Festival of Negro Arts and Culture.

  • 1982 - Enahoro was the chairman of the National Democratic Coalition (NADECO), a pro-democracy group that fought dictator Sani Abacha till Abacha's death. Enahoro was conferred with the national honour of Commander, Order of the Federal Republic, CFR, and was the chairman of the Movement for National Reformation, MNR, as well as the Pro-National Conference Organisation (PRONACO).

  • 1972 - He was awarded honorary DSC by the University of Benin

  • 1962 - During the crisis in the old Western region, he was detained along with other Action Group members. Accused of treason during the Awolowo alleged coup trial.

  • 1963 - Enahoro escaped via Ghana to the United Kingdom

  • 15 December 2010 - he died after a protracted battle with diabetes at his residence in Benin city.
